7 Day Gentle Movement Challenge

Important Disclaimer & Permission to Proceed

This program is for educational purposes only. You are responsible for your own safety. Only perform movements that feel safe and comfortable for you. Move slowly. Stop immediately if you feel pain, dizziness, or discomfort. By continuing, you acknowledge that you are choosing to participate at your own pace and within your personal limits.

Bridge

This exercise helps you get out of bed, stand upright, and protect your lower back.

Reps: 6–10 slow reps
One rep
Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor, close to your hips.
Arms rest by your sides. Relax your shoulders.
Press both feet gently into the floor.
Slowly lift your hips until your body feels comfortable, not forced.
Pause for one second at the top.
Slowly lower your hips back down until they fully rest on the floor.
Exhale as you lift. Inhale as you lower.
Only go as far as you comfortably can.

Cat–Cow Stretch

This exercise helps reduce stiffness in your spine and makes daily movement feel easier.

Reps: 6–10 slow rounds
One round
Start on hands and knees with hands under shoulders and knees under hips.
Inhale and gently arch your back, lifting your chest slightly. This is Cow.
Exhale and gently round your back, letting your head relax down. This is Cat.
Return to a neutral position. That completes one round.
Move slowly and smoothly with your breath.
Only go as far as you comfortably can.

Chair Sit-to-Stand

This exercise helps you stand up from chairs, couches, and toilets with more confidence.

Reps: 5–8 reps
One rep
Sit near the front edge of the chair with feet flat on the floor, hip width apart.
Lean your chest slightly forward.
Press through your heels and slowly stand up.
Stand tall without locking your knees.
Pause for one second.
Slowly lower yourself back down until fully seated.
Use hands on the chair if needed.
Only go as far as you comfortably can.

Heel Raises

This exercise helps improve balance and makes walking feel more stable.

Reps: 8–12 slow reps
One rep
Stand upright with feet hip width apart.
Lightly hold a wall or chair for balance if needed.
Slowly lift both heels so you rise onto the balls of your feet.
Pause for one second at the top.
Slowly lower your heels back to the floor until fully down.
Breathe naturally throughout the movement.
Only go as far as you comfortably can.

Wall Push-Ups

This exercise helps you push doors, counters, and yourself up safely using your arms.

Reps: 6–10 reps
One rep
Stand facing a wall and place hands on the wall at shoulder height.
Step feet back slightly so your body leans toward the wall.
Bend elbows and slowly bring your chest closer to the wall.
Stop when your arms feel comfortably bent.
Press the wall away and return to the starting position.
Move slowly and with control.
Only go as far as you comfortably can.
